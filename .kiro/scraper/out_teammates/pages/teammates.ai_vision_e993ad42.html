From Uktob → Teammates.ai

If you’ve been with us since our “Uktob.ai” days, you know we started with a B2C Generative AI platform that grew to 400,000+ users organically. We loved building Faheem, our AI assistant, and we saw firsthand the power of AI to scale rapidly, smash language barriers, and enrich everyday life, on some days, for 100K+ daily active users.

From the early days, we formed a thesis which crystallized over time:

01

AI isn’t just about creating content or assisting – it can autonomously run entire business functions — no human babysitting required.

02

The greatest value creation – might not be in selling more software; it’s in delegating entire job roles — tapping into the trillion-dollar labor budget rather than the mere $200B–$300B software budget.

03

By building AI “Teammates” that work alongside humans – scaling them, amplifying them, and freeing them to focus on strategy and innovation – organizations can scale exponentially.

Hence the rebrand: Teammates.ai. The name embodies our core belief that AI and humans, working together, can accomplish more than either could alone.

From Tools → Teammates

For decades, “enterprise software” forced humans to toggle between countless dashboards, spreadsheets, and guesswork. People ended up as the glue that holds half-baked tools together. We’re flipping that script:

Our AI Teammates do the heavy lifting.

We believe the true breakthrough lies in fully owning all the daily, labor-intensive tasks that bog down roles like a customer service rep, sales agent, or an onboarding specialist. At Teammates.ai, we don’t label them “agents” or “assistants.” We call them Teammates because:

They join your team – They log into your systems, handle your tickets or leads, and talk to customers via chat, phone, emails on any platform - taking actions from over 30+ native integrations we’ve built.

They work with you, not against you – We don’t want to replace humans. We want to free humans from mindless tasks so they can focus on truly human strengths — relationship-building, innovation, empathy.

But while Teammates are joining real-world teams and freeing human staff from the mundane, the rest of the industry often lumps together any AI-enabled system—chatbots, copilots, assistants—as if they’re all the same.

They’re not. We see countless half-baked “agents” that do a sliver of the job, demand constant human oversight, or merely add an AI veneer on top of existing tools. Meanwhile, our Teammates operate at the highest level of autonomy—tackling entire workflows end to end. So, before we dive deeper, let’s clarify exactly where we stand on the spectrum of AI evolution.

Network of Agents

Each one of our teammates is made up of a network of AI Agents that collaborate.

If you’re another AI startup reading this — we welcome the challenge. But understand: we’ve already built the synergy. We’re not about static chatbots or pure automation. We’re about unstoppable, collaborative AI that elevates entire organizations.

Why now? – The $4.6+ Trillion Opportunity

(Yes, Trillion)

Never before has AI been able to create, reason, and interact at a human level. This changes everything. Instead of paying for software seats, companies can shift budgets to AI Teammates that deliver real outcomes—closing deals, resolving tickets, etc.—all at a fraction of the cost.

Trillion-Dollar Labor vs. Small Software Budgets
Right now, businesses spend billions—sometimes tens of billions—on call centers, sales teams, and operational roles. AI Teammates let you move from paying for “software seats” to paying for results: resolved tickets, closed deals, etc.

Selected Market Factors:

01

Cost of Intelligence Is Exponentially Collapsing – AI models have been improving 10× each year for the last decade. Project that forward: by 2030, they could be 100,000 times better than they are in 2025.

02

Breakthroughs in Architectures - “Infinite memory”, multi-modal architectures, complex reasoning, huge context windows, orchestration, ‘self-improving AI’—these breakthroughs mean AI can now reason, plan, and act across entire workflows without forgetting a single detail.
And soon Teammates will build their own micro-tools for tasks we never explicitly trained them for.

03

Shrinking Adoption Timelines - The time it takes ground-breaking tech to proliferate has been consistently shrinking. The PC took 20 years to reach 50% of the population in the U.S. Internet took 12 years. Mobile took 6. GenAI did that in less than 3. How long will it take for Agents to proliferate in the workforce?

You will see it this year, and we’re leading it.

Imagine This…

01

The Minute You Submit a “Contact Us” Form

Rashed (Sales) instantly researches the prospect, determines whether to give them a call — in their dialect — to gauge interest, collect details, and (if qualified) schedule a follow-up with your top closer.

Juggles outbound lead generation, inbound qualification, and follow-up at scale—relieving your sales stars to focus on real closers, big deals, and strategic expansions.

02

The Moment a Customer Submits a Support Ticket

Raya (Customer Service) hunts for solutions in your knowledge base, takes action, crafts a personalized response, and closes out the ticket—no manual triaging. Real staff step in only for those special escalations.

Takes over repetitive inbound queries, multitasking across voice, email, chat, and more.

03

The Second a Deal is Final

Both Teammates update all relevant systems—billing, CRM, shipping — without any human messing with spreadsheets or copying data. Your ops team sleeps easy.

Sound radical? Great. Because that’s how you transform entire ops, not just “improve some KPI by 5%.”

“Wrapper, Right?”

We heard the cynicism: “Isn’t it just a wrapper around GPT?”

That’s like saying “Isn’t Airbnb just a wrapper on residential properties?”, “Isn’t Uber just a wrapper on taxis?”, or “Isn’t all software just a wrapper on databases?”

“Wouldn’t Google/MSFT eat your lunch?” Before you ask this question, remember that this applies to every single tech company out there – until it doesn’t. If this were true, Hertz/Avis would’ve built Uber, and CNN would’ve built Facebook.

Disruptive innovations rarely come from incumbents. We partnered with VCs who saw beyond the noise, who recognized that true autonomy involves orchestrating numerous specialized agents, advanced architectures, robust integrations, compliance, cultural nuance, and a million other details not solved by a single GPT call.

We’re grateful to have aligned with Hustle Fund, Beyond Capital, Access Bridge Ventures, Oraseya Capital and notable angels who deeply understood the generational impact of AI Teammates—rather than repeating the dismissive ‘it’s just a wrapper’ chorus.
